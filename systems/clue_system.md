# Clue System

## Purpose

Teach players how to survive.

---

# Design Philosophy

Clues should reward attention.

Players should become more skilled through knowledge.

Clue-based shortcuts are one of the few real advantages a player has against a monster that is otherwise just as fast as they are — see systems/escape_system.md.

---

# Example

## Minotaur

Player Learns:

"The Minotaur follows blood trails."

Escape Phase:

Blood trails become meaningful.

---

# Requirements

- Clues must be visible
- Clues must be understandable
- Clues must become useful later

---

# Open Questions

- How explicit is the connection between a learned clue and its in-game payoff — environmental only, or reinforced with a UI prompt?
- Should clues be mythology-specific only, or can some clue *types* (e.g. "look for unusual symbols") generalize across mythologies to teach a transferable skill?
- What happens if a player misses a clue entirely — is the level still completable, just harder?
