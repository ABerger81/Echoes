# Devlog v0.5.0

Date: 2026-06-20

## Goal

Death / Capture State — make capture a real game state: freeze the player, show feedback, and ensure win and capture are mutually exclusive.

---

## Completed

- CaptureHandler.cs — subscribes to OnTimerExpired and OnLevelComplete, disables FirstPersonController and Interactor on either outcome
- CaptureUI.cs — shows "You were captured." on timer expiry, stays on screen until restart (Milestone 6)
- systems/escape_system.md — updated to include Milestone 5 files and the full win/capture event flow

---

## Problems Encountered

- After Level complete, timer kept counting down and fired OnTimerExpired — capture message appeared on a won game. Fix: added `_isLevelOver` check to GameManager.Update() guard so the timer stops as soon as the level ends.
- After Level complete, player could still move and look. Fix: CaptureHandler subscribes to OnLevelComplete as well as OnTimerExpired — same HandleCapture() method, two trigger events.

---

## Lessons Learned

- `_isLevelOver` must be checked in Update() itself, not only inside TriggerLevelComplete() — the timer guard needs to know the level is over to stop counting.
- One method can subscribe to multiple events: HandleCapture() handles both win freeze and capture freeze without duplicating logic.
- Disabling a MonoBehaviour (FirstPersonController.enabled = false) stops its Update() and LateUpdate() cleanly — cleaner than trying to zero out input values manually.
